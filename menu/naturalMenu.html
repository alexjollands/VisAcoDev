Here ants have one mission: find food and return to the nest. The purpose behind this scenario is to show that ants lay pheromone along their route as they go.

The amount of pheromone on a path influences other ants - the more pheromone the more likely they will choose the path.

After a little while, the colony tends to converge onto the shorter path as the ants travelling along it go back and forth quicker than those on a more round-about route.

To simplify things for this demonstration, the graph has been constrained to only three distinct paths.

Each ant chooses which path to go along based on the average amount of pheromone along the whole path (with a bit of randomness thrown in).
